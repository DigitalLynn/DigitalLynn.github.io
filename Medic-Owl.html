Medic-Owl

The nurses of the Westmead ICU wards are extremely time poor and generally lack effective training and education. We were tasked to craft a solution

User pain points:

  • Nurses are perpetually in a duty of care
  • Unprodictable work environment
  • Inconsistent work environments

User needs:

Education

Nurses need satisfyinf educational traning that can help them learn each day

Flexibility

The need to allow interruption, and be able to pick up where they left off from

Relief

Learning tht isn’t mentally draining or straining and doesn’t feel like more work

Incentive

Motivation and wanting to learn and improve their knowledge

Learning

The learning modules were designed to have a 3 step process which limited how long the users were broken from their patient. The nurses are also given simple visual aids to support content for the nurses quickly absorb information. The predominant form is having case studies presented patients as comic-book characters to allow further reinforcement.

Competition


Nurses are encouraged to compete amongst themselves to increase the use of the app and promote greater cohesion in the workplace. Each month the team the nurse has chosen will be ranked against all others and the winning team can take the victory prize. The prizes can be awarded on a ward to ward basis to allow further personalisation.

Revision


The crux of the learning is the reinforcement of what the nurses have already learnt from previous training. An encyclopedia is available and the same learning tools as above are applied.

Flexibility


The app was designed to be used with an easy 1 to 5 minute window in which nurses, in a moment of downtime, could use the app and return to the patient without the quality of care decreases.
