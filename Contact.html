About Me

My name is Henry. I’m a second-year Design Computing student at the University of Sydney. I was born and raised as a Sydney urbanite enjoying the many facets of metropolitan life and growing into myself in the density of the city.

I am a burgeoning designer who seeks to apply the design mindset to the challenges we face in our daily lives. This manifests in making experiences that people want to use, not have to. Every iteration is a step closer to this goal.

I am always seeking to expand the tools I have at my disposal. Visually I have used Illustrator and InDesign to help communicate complex ideas in more digestible bites. For UI solutions I tend to favour Figma and Sketch for iterating low to medium fidelity prototypes, then making the final solution in XD. For VR development I favour Unity. I too always seek to improve my UX skills in understanding why people do what they do, even if they don’t know why.

To me, design is all about communication; listening and asking the user, the client or your fellow designers. This allows us to empathise with the users and understand the parameters of stakeholder all to better understand the problem. This breaks down our complex problems into a mass of simple ones, each one affecting the growth of our solution.

To find out more you may contact me or read my resume as provided.
