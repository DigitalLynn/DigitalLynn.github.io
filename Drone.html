Drön


Drön is a peer to peer drone delivery service which alleviates the inconvenience of making constant deliveries in person. Major pain points observed was that users they rarely wanted to deliver the object as more often then not there were more pressing errands that could be done. This left users dissatisfied and in need for a disruption.

Software

Design methods

  • Wireframing
  • User walkthrough

  • Think aloud

Design methods


Users expressed that they’d like to be able to see their contacts on the map, and differentiate between their contacts and themselves on the map.

The faces were diesigned to replicate social media's  and were designed to reduce the number of actions a user must take to complete their objective. When tapped, these plarques reveal a card with relevant information, and an option to begin a new delivery with that contact.

Design methods


Users were confused by the route information that was displayed on the delivery pages. In order to decrease confusion, we chose to display the entire route the drone will take at all times, and moved the ETA to the bottom of the screen. Motion will also be used in the final product to help the user understand the direction the drone will move.

The iconography was also improved to increase the differentiation of drone, sender and reciever

Design methods


Users usually don’t actually know the dimensions of their item - so asking them to select a drone based on size proved difficult.

Instead, we’ve created a slider, which will cycle through common items from smallest to largest that the drone can carry. This allows the correct size drone to be selected in the background, without the user having to know any dimensions.
