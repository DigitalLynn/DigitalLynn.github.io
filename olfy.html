Challenge

Priscilla came to me with an experimental device that allowed users to smell in VR (NDA'd so no further explanation) and wanted to develop a prototype to test and experiment with.

Approach - Discovery

Early on we established several soft rules for the narrative we would make:
~ No words
~ Short
~ For children
Australian children grow up on a unique series of picture books that focus on our unique nature. The natural environment gave good grounding for the smells and setting for the narrative.

Approach - Storyboarding

After deciding and storyboarding a narrative, Priscilla and I itterated the story for children decideding how best to facilitate the story in VR.
~ A series of sketches to quickly ideate.
~ Traditional story board for refining the narrative.
~ A VR storyboard to clarify positioning and movement.
Ultimately the story revolved around two characters having a simple interaction and exploring the space together to give the user ample oportunity to smell the environment.

Approach - Rapid Prototyping

Working with Mikkel, we built the scene to reflect a child's fantasy land while working within the constraints and opportunities of a VR narrative. My roles included:
~ Positioning of users and characters
~ Sound of diegtic game objects
~ Animation of characters
Mikkel developed the aesthetic design of the scene, especially to appeal to children.

Outcome and Reflection

As my first narrative in VR there are many things that I would differently that could only be realised with hindsight. Primarily would be to reposition the user so the experience takes place in and around them rather than directly in front. Secondly, I would have multiple points of interest where I could create simultaneous stories unfold rather than follow a single point of interest like the user is a camera. Experimenting and breaking away from these old habits from the film world will be a new and interesting challenge going forward.
